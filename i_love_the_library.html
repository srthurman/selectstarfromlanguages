I love the library

Mon 25 January 2016
By Sara Thurman

Hey kids, and adults, have you checked out (pun intended) your local library's offerings lately? I don't mean what new books they've added to the shelves, but their digital offerings? If not, you might be missing out on some really great resources.

The District of Columbia Public Library (DCPL) has a few offerings which I'm particularly excited about.

Lynda.com

Through DCPL, you can log into Lynda.com and have unlimited access to all their resources! It's a big resource, with lots of new materials added regularly.

Safari Books Online

Some of the offerings are admittedly a bit dated, at least by standards of software development, but Safari Books Online is still a good place to find books on a range of tech topics.

Universal Class

I haven't actually used Universal Class, just browsed the courses, It has a pretty wide range of topics covered, and I've got a few earmarked to try out.

Click here to see the full range of offerings from DCPL.

That's all for now. Be sure to check out your local library to see what subscriptions they make available. Have fun learning!
